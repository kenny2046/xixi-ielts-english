PLAY IS A SERIOUS BUSINESS Does play help develop bigger, better brains? Bryony Furton investigates
READING PASSAGE 3

A Playing is a serious business. Children engrossed in a make-believe world, kittens chasing a ball of string aren't just having fun. Play may look like a carefree and exuberant way to pass the time before the hard work of adulthood comes along, but there's much more to it than that. For a start, play can cost animal their lives. Eighty per cent of deaths among young fur seals for instance occur because playing pups fail to spot predators approach- ing. It is also extremely expensive in terms of energy. Playful young animals use around two or three per cent of their energy diverting, and in children that figure can be closer to fifteen per cent. 'There must be a reason for it,' says John Byers of Idaho University. 'You just don't find animals wasting energy like that,' he adds. There must be a reason.Q29Q32Q38
游玩是一项严肃的事业。沉浸在幻想世界中的孩子，或者追逐线球的小猫，并不仅仅是在玩乐。游玩看起来可能是在成年期的艰苦工作到来之前，一种无忧无虑且充满活力的消磨时间的方式，但其实远不止于此。首先，游玩可能会让动物付出生命的代价。例如，年轻毛皮海豹的死亡中有80%是因为玩耍的幼崽未能发现捕食者的接近。此外，在能量方面，它的消耗也极其巨大。玩耍的幼年动物会消耗大约2%到3%的能量，而在儿童中，这一比例可能接近15%。爱达荷大学的约翰·拜尔斯说：“这其中一定有原因。”他补充道，“你不会发现动物会这样浪费能量。这其中必然有原因。”
B But if play is not simply a developmental hiccup, a harmless excess of religious energy, why did it evolve? The latest idea suggests that play has evolved to build big brains. In other words, playing makes you intelligent. Play, it seems, is common only among mammals, although a few of the larger-brained birds also indulge. Animals at play often use unique signs – tail- wagging in dogs, for example - to indicate that activity superficially resembling adult behaviour is not really in earnest. A popular explanation of play has been that it helps juveniles develop the skills they will need to hunt, mate and survive as adults. Another was that it allows young animals to get in shape for adult life by improving their respiratory endurance. Both these ideas have been questioned in recent years.Q32Q33Q40
但如果游玩不仅仅是一个发育过程中的小插曲，或者某种无害的宗教能量过剩，那它为什么要进化呢？最新的观点认为，游玩是为了构建大脑而进化的。换句话说，游玩让你变得聪明。游玩似乎只在哺乳动物中常见，尽管一些大脑较大的鸟类也会参与其中。玩耍中的动物经常会使用独特的信号——例如狗摇尾巴——来表明这种表面上模仿成年行为的活动并非真的在认真进行。一种关于游玩的流行解释是，它帮助青少年发展成年后狩猎、交配和生存所需的技能。另一种观点是，它通过提高呼吸耐力，让幼年动物为成年生活做好体能准备。这两个观点近年来都受到了质疑。
C Take the exercise theory. If play evolved to build muscle or as a kind of endurance training, then you would expect to see permanent benefits. But Byers points out that the benefits of increased exercise disappear rapidly after training stops, so any improvement in endurance resulting from juvenile play would be lost by adulthood. 'If the function of play was to get into shape,' says Byers, 'the optimum time for playing would depend on when it was most advantageous for the young of a particular species to do so. But it doesn't work like that.' Across species, play tends to peak about halfway through the suckling stage and then decline.Q33Q38
以锻炼理论为例。如果游玩的进化是为了增强肌肉或作为一种耐力训练，那么你应该能看到永久性的益处。但拜尔斯指出，运动量增加带来的益处在训练停止后会迅速消失，因此青少年时期玩耍所带来的耐力提升到成年时就会丧失。拜尔斯说：“如果游玩的功能是为了健身，那么最佳游玩时间应该取决于对特定物种的幼崽最有利的时候。但事实并非如此。”在各物种中，游玩往往在哺乳期的中间阶段达到巅峰，然后开始下降。
D Then there's the skills-training hypothesis. At first glance, playing animals do appear to be practising the complex manoeuvres they will need in adulthood. But a closer inspection reveals this interpretation as too simplistic. In one study, behavioural scientist Tim Caro, from the University of California, looked at the predatory play of kittens and their predatory behaviour when they reached adulthood. He found that the way the cats played had no significant effect on their hunting prowess in later life.Q33
然后是技能训练假说。乍一看，玩耍中的动物确实表现出在练习成年后所需的复杂动作。但进一步观察会发现这种解释过于简单。在一项研究中，来自加州大学的行为科学家蒂姆·卡罗观察了幼猫的捕食性游戏以及它们成年后的捕食行为。他发现，猫玩耍的方式对其后期生活中的狩猎能力没有显著影响。
E Earlier this year, Sergio Pellis of Lethbridge University, Canada, reported that there is a strong positive link between brain size and playfulness among mammals in general. Comparing measurements for fifteen orders of mammal, he and his team found that larger brains (for a given body size) are linked to greater playfulness. The converse was also found to be true. Robert Barton of Durham University believes that, because large brains are more sensitive to developmental stimuli than smaller brains, they require more play to help mould them for adulthood. 'I concluded it's to do with learning, and with the importance of environmental cues to the brain during development,' he says.Q37Q39
今年早些时候，加拿大莱斯布里奇大学的塞尔吉奥·佩利斯报告称，在哺乳动物中，大脑大小与玩耍程度之间存在极强的正相关。通过比较15个哺乳动物目的测量数据，他和他的团队发现，（在特定身体尺寸下）较大的大脑与更高的玩耍程度相关。反之亦然。杜伦大学的罗伯特·巴顿认为，由于大脑越大对发育刺激越敏感，因此它们需要更多的游玩来帮助塑造其成年状态。他说：“我的结论是，这与学习有关，也与发育过程中环境线索对大脑的重要性有关。”
F According to Byers, the timing of the playful stage in young animals provides an important clue to what's going on. If you plot the amount of time a juvenile devotes to play each day over the course of its development, you discover a pattern typically associated with a 'sensitive period' - a brief development window during which the brain can actually be modified in ways that are not possible earlier or later in life. Think of the relative ease with which young children – but not infants or adults – master language. Other researchers have found that play in cats, rats and mice is at its most intense just as this 'window of opportunity' reaches its peak.Q28Q38
根据拜尔斯的说法，幼年动物玩耍阶段的时间点为正在发生的事情提供了重要线索。如果你描绘出青少年在发育过程中每天投入游玩的时间量，你会发现一种通常与“敏感期”相关的模式——这是一个短暂的发育窗口，在此期间，大脑实际上可以以生命早期或后期都不可能实现的方式被修改。想想幼儿——而非婴儿或成年人——掌握语言的相对容易程度。其他研究人员发现，猫、大鼠和小鼠的游玩在这一“机会窗口”达到巅峰时也最为激烈。
G 'People have not paid enough attention to the amount of the brain activated by play,' says Marc Bekoff from Colorado University. Bekoff studied coyote pups at play and found that the kind of behaviour involved was markedly more complex and subdivided than that of adults. Such behaviour activates many different parts of the brain, he reasons. Bekoff likens it to a behavioural kaleidoscope, with animals at play jumping rapidly between activities. 'They use behaviour from a lot of different contexts – predation, aggression, reproduction,' he says. 'Their developing brain is getting all sorts of stimulation.'Q30Q38
科罗拉多大学的马克·贝考夫说：“人们还没有足够关注游玩所激活的大脑区域数量。”贝考夫研究了玩耍中的小土狼，发现其中涉及的行为比成年的行为明显更复杂且细分。他推论，这种行为激活了大脑的许多不同部分。贝考夫将其比作行为万花筒，玩耍中的动物在各项活动之间快速跳跃。他说：“它们使用了来自许多不同背景的行为——捕食、攻击、繁殖。它们发育中的大脑正在接受各种刺激。”
H Not only is more of the brain involved in play than was suspected, but it also seems to activate higher cognitive processes. 'There's enormous cognitive involvement in play,' says Bekoff. He points out that play often involves complex assessments of playmates, ideas of reciprocity and the use of specialised signals and rules. He believes that play creates a brain that has greater behavioural flexibility and improved potential for learning later in life. The idea is backed up by the work of Stephen Siviy of Gettysburg College. Siviy studied how hours of play affected the brain's levels of a particular chemical associated with the stimulation and growth of nerve cells. He was surprised by the extent of the activation. 'Play lights everything up,' he says. By allowing link-ups between brain areas that might not normally communicate with each other, play may enhance creativity.Q27Q30Q40
参与游玩的大脑区域不仅比预想的要多，而且似乎还激活了更高级的认知过程。贝考夫说：“游玩中存在巨大的认知参与。”他指出，游玩通常涉及对玩伴的复杂评估、互惠理念以及特殊信号和规则的使用。他认为，游玩创造了一个具有更大行为灵活性和提高后期学习潜能的大脑。这一观点得到了盖茨堡学院史蒂芬·西维的研究支持。西维研究了游玩时长如何影响大脑中一种与神经细胞刺激和生长相关的特定化学物质水平。他对激活的程度感到惊讶。他说：“游玩点亮了一切。”通过允许通常互不沟通的大脑区域之间建立联系，游玩可能会增强创造力。
I What might further experimentation suggest about the way children are raised in many societies today? We already know that rat pups denied the chance to play grow smaller brain components and fail to develop the ability to apply social rules when they interact with their peers. With schooling beginning earlier and becoming increasingly exam-oriented, play is likely to get even less of a look-in. Who knows what the result of that will be!Q31
进一步的实验可能会对当今许多社会的儿童养育方式产生什么启示？我们已经知道，被剥夺游玩机会的小鼠幼崽大脑组件会长得较小，并且在与同伴互动时无法发展出应用社会规则的能力。随着学校教育开始得更早且日益趋向考试导向，游玩的机会可能会变得更少。谁知道那会导致什么结果呢！
