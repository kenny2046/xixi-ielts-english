ALTERNATIVE MEDICINE IN AUSTRALIA
READING PASSAGE 2
You should spend about 20 minutes on Questions 14–26, which are based on Reading Passage 2 below.
你应该用大约 20 分钟完成 14–26 题，题目基于下方的阅读文章 2。
The first students to study alternative medicine at university level in Australia began their four-year, full-time course at the University of Technology, Sydney, in early 1994. Their course covered, among other therapies, acupuncture. The irony they have to face is that traditional Chinese explanations of how acupuncture works are that it can regulate the flow of 'Qi' or energy through pathways in the body. This concept explains how some alternative therapies have stood in their struggle for acceptance by the scientific establishment.
1994年初，澳大利亚首批在大学层面学习替代疗法的学生在悉尼科技大学开始了为期四年的全日制课程。他们的课程涵盖了包括针灸在内的多种疗法。他们不得不面对的讽刺局面是，传统中医对针灸原理的解释是它可以调节体内“气”或能量的流动。这一概念解释了一些替代疗法在争取科学界认可的斗争中所处的立场。
Australia has been unusual in the Western world in having a very conservative attitude to natural or alternative therapies, according to Dr Paul Laver, a lecturer in Public Health at the University of Sydney. 'We've had a tradition of doctors being fairly powerful and I guess they are pretty loath to allow any pretenders to their position to come into it.' In many other industrialised countries, orthodox and alternative medicine have worked 'hand in glove' for years. In Europe, only orthodox doctors can prescribe herbal medicine. In Germany, plant remedies account for 10% of the national turnover of pharmaceuticals. Americans made more visits to alternative therapists than to orthodox doctors in 1990, and each year they spend about $US 12 billion on therapies that have not been scientifically tested.Q14Q15Q17
悉尼大学公共卫生讲师保罗·拉弗博士表示，澳大利亚在西方世界中对天然或替代疗法持非常保守的态度，这一点很不寻常。“我们这里的医生一直拥有相当大的权力，我想他们非常不愿让任何觊觎其地位的人进入这个领域。”在许多其他工业化国家，正统医学和替代医学已经“紧密配合”多年。在欧洲，只有正统医生可以开草药处方。在德国，植物药占全国药物营业额的10%。1990年，美国人向替代疗法治疗师咨询的次数超过了向正统医生咨询的次数，且每年花费约120亿美元用于未经科学验证的疗法。
Disenchantment with orthodox medicine has seen the popularity of alternative therapies in Australia climb steadily during the past 20 years. In a 1983 national health survey, 1.9% of people said they had contacted a chiropractor, naturopath, osteopath, acupuncturist or herbalist in the two weeks prior to the survey. By 1990, this figure had risen to 2.6% of the population. The 550,000 consultations with alternative therapists reported in the 1990 survey represented about an eighth of the total number of consultations with medically qualified personnel covered by the survey, according to Dr Laver and colleagues writing in the Australian Journal of Public Health in 1993. A better educated and less accepting public has become disillusioned with the experts in general, and increasingly sceptical about science and empirically based knowledge,' they said. 'The high standing of professionals, including doctors, has been eroded as a consequence.'Q16Q18Q19
对正统医学的幻灭使得替代疗法在澳大利亚的受欢迎程度在过去20年里稳步上升。在1983年的一项全国健康调查中，1.9%的人表示他们在调查前的两周内联系过脊椎按摩师、自然疗法师、整骨师、针灸师或草药师。到1990年，这一比例已上升至总人口的2.6%。根据拉弗博士及其同事1993年在《澳大利亚公共卫生杂志》上发表的文章，1990年调查中报告的55万次替代疗法咨询约占调查所涵盖的具有医疗资质人员咨询总数的八分之一。他们表示：“受教育程度更高、更不盲从的公众普遍对专家感到失望，对科学和基于经验的知识也日益怀疑。因此，包括医生在内的专业人士的高地位已被削弱。”
Rather than resisting or criticising this trend, increasing numbers of Australian doctors, particularly younger ones, are forming group practices with alternative therapists or taking courses themselves, particularly in acupuncture and herbalism. Part of the incentive was financial, Dr Laver said. 'The bottom line is that most general practitioners are a bit needy. If they see patients are going elsewhere, they might want to be able to offer a similar service.'Q20Q21
越来越多的澳大利亚医生，尤其是年轻医生，并没有抵制或批评这一趋势，而是正与替代疗法师合作开展联合诊疗，或者亲自修读课程，尤其是针灸和草药学。拉弗博士说，其中的一部分动力来自财务方面。“底线是大多数全科医生都有些拮据。如果他们看到患者流向别处，他们可能也想能够提供类似的服务。”
In 1993, Dr Laver and his colleagues published a survey of 289 Sydney people who attended eight alternative therapists' practices in Sydney. These practices offered a wide range of alternative therapies from 25 therapists. Those surveyed had experienced chronic illnesses, for which orthodox medicine had been able to provide little relief. They commented that they liked the holistic approach of their alternative therapists and the friendly, concerned and detailed attention they had received. The cold, impersonal manner of orthodox doctors was a feature in the survey. An increasing number of patients, consulted with this and a number of other relevant surveys carried out in Australia, all pointing to orthodox doctors' inadequacies, have led mainstream doctors themselves to begin to admit they could learn a lot from the personal style of alternative therapists. Dr Patrick Store, President of the Royal College of General Practitioners, concedes that orthodox doctors could learn a lot about bedside manner and advising patients on preventative health from alternative therapists.Q22Q23
1993年，拉弗博士及其同事发表了一项针对悉尼8家替代疗法诊所的289名就诊者的调查。这些诊所由25名治疗师提供广泛的替代疗法。受访者都患有慢性疾病，正统医学对此几乎无法缓解。他们评论说，他们喜欢替代疗法师的整体疗法，以及他们所受到的友好、关怀且详尽的关注。正统医生冷漠、缺乏人情味的态度是调查中的一个特征。随着包括该调查在内的多项在澳相关调查都指向正统医生的不足，越来越多的患者使得主流医生自己也开始承认，他们可以从替代疗法师的个人风格中学习很多。皇家全科医生学院院长帕特里克·斯道尔博士承认，正统医生可以从替代疗法师那里学到很多关于临床态度和建议患者预防性健康的知识。
According to the Australian Journal of Public Health, 18% of patients visiting alternative therapists do so because they suffer from musculo-skeletal complaints; 12% suffer from digestive problems, which is only 1% more than those suffering from emotional problems. Those suffering from respiratory complaints represent 7% of their patients, and candida sufferers represent an equal percentage. Headache sufferers and those complaining of general ill health represent 6% and 5% of patients respectively, and a further 4% see therapists for general health maintenance.Q24Q25Q26
根据《澳大利亚公共卫生杂志》，在访问替代疗法师的患者中，18%是因为患有肌肉骨骼疾病；12%患有消化系统问题，这仅比患有情绪问题的人多1%。呼吸系统疾病患者占其患者的7%，念珠菌病患者占比相同。头痛患者和主诉身体普遍不适的患者分别占6%和5%，另有4%的人看治疗师是为了进行一般健康维护。
The survey suggested that complementary medicine is probably a better term than alternative medicine. Alternative medicine appears to be an adjunct, sought in times of disenchantment when conventional medicine seems not to offer the answer.
调查显示，“补充医学”可能比“替代医学”是一个更好的术语。替代医学似乎是一种辅助手段，在人们对传统医学感到幻灭、似乎无法提供答案时被寻求。
