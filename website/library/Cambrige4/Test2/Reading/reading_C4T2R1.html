Lost for Words Many minority languages are on the danger list
READING PASSAGE 1
You should spend about 20 minutes on Questions 1–13 which are based on Reading Passage 1 below.
你应该用大约20分钟的时间完成基于阅读文章1的第1-13题。
In the Native American Navajo nation, which spreads across four states in the American southwest, the native language is dying. Most of its speakers are middle-aged or elderly. Although many students take classes in Navajo, the schools are run in English. Street signs, supermarket goods and even their own newspaper are all in English. Not surprisingly, linguists doubt that any native speakers of Navajo will remain in a hundred years' time.Q10
在横跨美国西南部四个州的纳瓦霍原住民部落中，母语正在消亡。大多数说这种语言的人都是中年人或老年人。尽管许多学生选修纳瓦霍语课程，但学校是用英语运作的。路标、超市商品甚至他们自己的报纸都是英文的。不出所料，语言学家怀疑一百年后是否还会有任何以纳瓦霍语为母语的人存在。
Navajo is far from alone. Half the world's 6,800 languages are likely to vanish within two generations — that's one language lost every ten days. Never before has the planet's linguistic diversity shrunk at such a pace. 'At the moment, we are heading for about three or four languages dominating the world,' says Mark Pagel, an evolutionary biologist at the University of Reading. 'It's a mass extinction, and whether we will ever rebound from the loss is difficult to know.'Q1Q2Q7Q13
纳瓦霍语绝非个例。世界现存的6800种语言中，有一半可能在两代人之内消失——这意味着每十天就有一种语言消失。地球语言多样性的缩减从未达到过如此之快的速度。雷丁大学的进化生物学家马克·佩格尔表示：“目前，我们正趋向于三到四种语言统治世界的局面。这是一场大灭绝，我们是否能从这种损失中恢复过来尚不可知。”
Isolation breeds linguistic diversity: as a result, the world is peppered with languages spoken by only a few people. Only 250 languages have more than a million speakers, and at least 3,000 have fewer than 2,500. It is not necessarily these small languages that are about to disappear. Navajo is considered endangered despite having 150,000 speakers. What makes a language endangered is not just the number of speakers, but how old they are. If it is spoken by children it is relatively safe. The critically endangered languages are those that are only spoken by the elderly, according to Michael Krauss, director of the Alaska Native Language Center, in Fairbanks.Q1Q3Q5Q11
隔离孕育了语言的多样性：结果，世界上到处都是只有少数人使用的语言。只有250种语言的语言人数超过一百万，至少有3000种语言的使用人数不足2500人。濒临消失的并不一定是这些小语种。尽管纳瓦霍语有15万使用者，但它仍被认为处于濒危状态。决定一种语言是否濒危的不仅是使用者的人数，还有他们的年龄。如果儿童在使用它，它就相对安全。阿拉斯加大学费尔班克斯分校原住民语言中心主任迈克尔·克劳斯认为，极度濒危的语言是那些只有老年人使用的语言。
Why do people reject the language of their parents? It begins with a crisis of confidence, when a small community finds itself alongside a larger, wealthier society, says Nicholas Ostler, of Britain's Foundation for Endangered Languages, in Bath. 'People lose faith in their culture,' he says. 'When the next generation reaches their teens, they might not want to be induced into the old traditions.'Q8Q9
为什么人们会拒绝父母的语言？英国濒危语言基金会的尼古拉斯·奥斯特勒表示，这始于一种信心危机，当一个小社群发现自己处于一个更大、更富裕的社会旁边时，“人们对自己的文化失去了信心，”他说，“当下一代进入青少年时期，他们可能不想被引导进入旧的传统。”
The change is not always voluntary. Quite often, governments try to kill off a minority language by banning its use in public or discouraging its use in schools, all to promote national unity.Q2Q12
这种改变并不总是自愿的。通常情况下，政府会通过禁止在公共场合使用或不鼓励在学校使用少数民族语言来试图消灭它们，这一切都是为了促进国家统一。
The former US policy of running Indian reservation schools in English, for example, effectively put languages such as Navajo on the danger list. But Salikoko Mufwene, who chairs the Linguistics Department at the University of Chicago, argues that the deadliest weapon is not government policy but economic globalisation. 'Native Americans have lost pride in their language, but they have had to adapt to socio-economic pressures,' he says. 'They cannot refuse to speak English if most commercial activity is in English.' But are languages worth saving? At the very least, there is a loss of data for the study of languages and their evolution, which relies on comparisons between languages, both living and dead. When an unwritten and unrecorded language disappears, it is lost to science.Q6Q9
例如，美国以前在印第安保留地学校实行英语教学的政策，实际上将纳瓦霍语等语言列入了危险名单。但芝加哥大学语言学系主任萨利科科·穆夫韦内认为，最致命的武器不是政府政策，而是经济全球化。他说：“美洲原住民失去了对语言的自豪感，但他们不得不适应社会经济压力。如果大多数商业活动都是用英语进行的，他们就不能拒绝说英语。”但是，语言值得拯救吗？至少，对于研究语言及其进化的学科来说，这是一种数据的损失，因为这项研究依赖于现存和已消亡语言之间的比较。当一种没有文字、没有记录的语言消失时，它对科学来说就永远失去了。
Language is also intimately bound up with culture, so it may be difficult to preserve one without the other. 'If a person shifts from Navajo to English, they lose something,' Mufwene says. 'Moreover, the loss of diversity may also deprive us of different ways of looking at the world,' says Pagel. There is mounting evidence that learning a language produces physiological changes in the brain. 'Your brain and mine are different from the brain of someone who speaks French, for instance,' Pagel says, and this could affect our thoughts and perceptions. 'The patterns and connections we make among various concepts may be structured by the linguistic habits of our community.'Q7
语言也与文化紧密相连，因此很难在不保留其中之一的情况下保留另一个。穆夫韦内说：“如果一个人从纳瓦霍语转向英语，他们会失去一些东西。”此外，佩格尔说：“多样性的丧失也可能剥夺我们观察世界的不同方式。”越来越多的证据表明，学习一种语言会引起大脑的生理变化。佩格尔说：“例如，你我的大脑与说法语的人的大脑是不同的，”这可能会影响我们的想法和感知。“我们在各种概念之间建立的模式和联系可能是由我们社区的语言习惯构建的。”
So despite linguists' best efforts, many languages will disappear over the next century. But a growing interest in cultural identity may prevent the direst predictions from coming true. 'The key to fostering diversity is for people to learn their ancestral tongues, as well as the dominant language,' says Doug Whalen, founder and president of the Endangered Language Fund in New Haven, Connecticut. 'Most of these languages will not survive without a large degree of bilingualism,' he says. In New Zealand, classes for children have slowed the erosion of Maori and rekindled interest in the language. A similar approach in Hawaii has produced about 8,000 new speakers of Polynesian languages in the past few years. In California, 'apprentice' programmes have provided life support to several indigenous languages. Volunteer 'apprentices' pair up with one of the last living speakers of a Native American tongue to learn a traditional skill such as basket weaving, with instruction exclusively in the endangered language. After about 300 hours of training, they are generally sufficiently fluent to transmit the language to the next generation. But Mufwene says that preserving a language dying out is not the same as giving it new life by using it every day: 'Preserving a language is more like preserving fruits in a jar,' he says.Q4Q6
因此，尽管语言学家做出了最大努力，许多语言仍将在下个世纪消失。但人们对文化认同感日益增长的兴趣可能会阻止最可怕的预言成真。康涅狄格州纽黑文濒危语言基金会的创始人兼总裁道格·惠伦说：“促进多样性的关键在于人们学习他们的祖先语言以及主流语言。”他说：“如果不实现高度的双语化，这些语言中的大多数都无法生存。”在新西兰，针对儿童的课程减缓了毛利语的流失，并重新燃起了人们对该语言的兴趣。夏威夷的类似方法在过去几年中培养了大约8000名波利尼西亚语的新使用者。在加利福尼亚，“学徒”计划为几种土著语言提供了生命支持。志愿者“学徒”与美洲原住民语言的最后几位使用者之一结对，学习篮筐编织等传统技能，教学完全使用濒危语言。经过大约300小时的训练，他们通常能够流利地将语言传给下一代。但穆夫韦内说，保护一种正在消亡的语言并不等同于通过每天使用它来赋予它新的生命：“保护一种语言更像是把水果保存在罐子里，”他说。
However, preservation can bring a language back from the dead. There are examples of languages that have survived in written form and then been restored by later generations. But a written record is essential for this, so the possibility of revival has led many speakers of endangered languages to develop systems of writing where none existed before.
然而，保护可以让一种语言起死回生。有一些语言以书面形式幸存下来，然后被后代恢复的例子。但书面记录对此至关重要，因此复兴的可能性促使许多濒危语言的使用者开发了以前不存在的文字系统。
