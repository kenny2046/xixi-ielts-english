你应该用大约20分钟的时间回答基于阅读文章1的第1-14题。
1 Adults and children are frequently confronted with statements about the alarming rate of loss of tropical rainforests. For example, one graphic illustration to which children might readily relate is the estimate that rainforests are being destroyed at a rate equivalent to one thousand football fields every forty minutes – about the duration of a normal lunchtime. In the face of the frequent and often vivid media coverage, it is likely that children will have formed ideas about rainforests – what and where they are, why they are important, what endangers them – independent of any formal tuition. It is also possible that some of these ideas will be mistaken.Q1Q2
成人和儿童经常面临有关热带雨林流失速度惊人的陈述。例如，一个孩子们可能很容易联系到的生动插图是，据估计，热带雨林正以每40分钟（大约相当于一顿普通午餐的时间）一千个足球场速度被破坏。面对频繁且往往生动的媒体报道，孩子们很可能在没有任何正式教学的情况下，就已经形成了关于热带雨林的观点——它们是什么、在哪里、为什么重要、什么威胁着它们。同样，其中一些观点也可能是错误的。
2 Many studies have shown that children harbour misconceptions about 'pure', curriculum science. These misconceptions do not remain isolated but become incorporated into a multifaceted, but organised, conceptual framework, making it and the component ideas, some of which are erroneous, more robust but also harder to modify. These ideas need to be identified and addressed if teachers are to develop programmes that help children refine or replace these child-centred conceptions with more conventional scientific ideas.Q3Q4
许多研究表明，孩子们对“纯粹的”课程科学存在误解。这些误解并非孤立存在，而是被纳入一个多层面但有组织的逻辑框架中，使得该框架及其组成观点（其中一些是错误的）更加稳固，但也更难修改。如果教师要制定计划帮助孩子们完善或用更传统的科学观点取代这些以孩子为中心的观念，就必须识别并解决这些想法。
3 Despite the abundance of information regarding the prediction of rainforests, little is known about children's ideas about this issue. The aim of the present study is to start to provide such information, to help teachers design educational programmes to help children reach an understanding of the importance of rainforests and their role in helping to maintain environmental stability.Q7
尽管关于预测雨林的信息非常丰富，但人们对孩子们关于这一问题的看法却知之甚少。本研究的目的是开始提供此类信息，以帮助教师设计教育课程，帮助学生理解雨林的重要性以及它们在维持环境稳定方面的作用。
4 The study surveys children's scientific knowledge and attitudes to rainforests. The sample consists of secondary school children aged 11-15 years from two schools in central England. The schools were chosen to represent the range of such schools in the UK. The children were asked to complete a questionnaire containing five open-form questions. The most frequent responses to the first question in the questionnaire, which asked what a rainforest is, in terms of geographic location by continent or country, were Central America (44%), South America (43%), Brazil (31%). Some children also gave more general locations, such as being near the Equator.Q5Q9
该研究调查了儿童对热带雨林的科学知识和态度。样本由来自英格兰中部两所学校的11-15岁中学生组成。选择这些学校是为了代表英国此类学校的范围。孩子们被要求完成一份包含五个开放式问题的问卷。问卷第一个问题是询问什么是热带雨林，就大陆或国家的地理位置而言，最常见的回答是中美洲（44%）、南美洲（43%）和巴西（31%）。一些孩子还给出了更笼统的位置，比如在赤道附近。
5 Responses to a question of why rainforests are important tended to fall into two main categories: animal habitats and the provision of oxygen for the planet. A smaller number of children stated that the rainforests provided medicines for humanity, and even fewer mentioned the indigenous populations of the forests. These results cluster around three basic ideas: rainforests as a location, a home and a resource.Q6Q10
关于雨林为何重要的问题，回答往往分为两大类：动物栖息地和为地球提供氧气。少数儿童表示，热带雨林为人类提供药物，提到森林中土著居民的人就更少了。这些结果围绕着三个基本理念：雨林作为地理位置、家园和资源。
6 Similarly, but at a deeper level, many students said that they thought that rainforests provided us with clean air. These students seemed to represent the oxygen as a sort of pure air, free from pollution. Some of these children even suggested that the oxygen/clean air given out by the plants was to compensate for the air pollution caused by car exhausts and factory emissions as a way of keeping the air pure and healthy.Q11Q13
同样，但在更深层次上，许多学生表示，他们认为热带雨林为我们提供了清洁的空气。这些学生似乎将氧气表现为一种纯净的、没有污染的空气。其中一些孩子甚至建议，植物排放的氧气/清洁空气是为了补偿汽车尾气和工厂排放造成的空气污染，作为保持空气纯净健康的一种方式。
7 The last two questions on the questionnaire were about what causes of rainforest destruction and the impact this would have on the planet respectively. In the first instance, the responses were divided between those who mentioned global activities (such as mining and drilling) and those who mentioned more localized activities (such as farming and ranching). Many of these children also linked the idea of rainforest destruction with that of pollution, identifying that it was common to see human-made pollution as responsible for the destruction of the environment. In particular, a smaller proportion said that pollution, a destroying and heating force, which children saw as coming from cars and factories, would damage or even destroy the rainforests. With this belief, there was clear evidence that children can think of climate change, specifically the greenhouse effect, as a cause of rainforest destruction. At the same time, children often referred to the ozone layer as being destroyed by the same pollutants, and that this would cause more heat to penetrate the earth from the sun.Q12Q13
调查问卷的最后两个问题分别是关于雨林破坏的原因及其对地球的影响。在第一种情况下，回答分为提到全球活动（如采矿和钻探）的人和提到更局部活动（如农业和牧场）的人。这些孩子中的许多人还将雨林破坏的想法与污染联系起来，认为看到人为污染对环境破坏负责是很常见的。特别是有较小比例的人说，污染——一种破坏和加热的力量，孩子们认为这种力量来自汽车和工厂——会破坏甚至摧毁热带雨林。有了这种信念，就有明确的证据表明，孩子们可以将气候变化，特别是温室效应，视为雨林破坏的一个原因。与此同时，孩子们经常提到臭氧层正被同样的污染物破坏，这将导致更多的热量从太阳渗透到地球。
8 In answer to the final question on the impact on the planet, most children felt that the destruction of the rainforests would lead to global warming and the melting of the ice caps. Only a few children mentioned the effect on the atmosphere or on water cycles. This is not surprising, considering the high level of media coverage on the ice caps. Most of the responses to the last two questions were based on the idea of the rainforest as a location, which would then be destroyed by global warming and the loss of the ozone layer.Q8Q13
在回答关于对地球影响的最后一个问题时，大多数孩子认为热带雨林的破坏会导致全球变暖和冰盖融化。只有少数孩子提到了对大气或水循环的影响。考虑到媒体对冰盖的高度关注，这并不奇怪。对最后两个问题的大部分回答都基于雨林作为一个地理位置的概念，然后这个位置会被全球变暖和臭氧层的丧失所摧毁。
9 The results of this study suggest that teachers need to consider the thinking of children about rainforests. Simple responses indicate how some ideas are incorporated in both scientific knowledge and also their concerns such as their ideas about the link between scientific literacy in plants and humans and the relationship between climate change and the loss of habitats.Q14
这项研究的结果表明，教师需要考虑孩子们对热带雨林的思考。简单的回答表明了一些想法是如何融入科学知识以及他们的担忧之中的，比如他们关于动植物科学素养与人类之间联系的想法，以及气候变化与栖息地丧失之间的关系。
10 Pupils from other countries are also expected to have developed a cognitive model of what a rainforest is. Like their UK counterparts, they grow and develop in a environment of ideas. Some types of environmental awareness are important and can be used to construct a solid basis for environmental education. It is clear that teachers must realize that their students already come into class with ideas that are not always correct and that some of these ideas will be difficult to change. It is recommended that some elements of environmental education should start at a much younger age, so that children can have a better understanding of their environment.Q14
来自其他国家的学生也被期望已经建立了一个关于什么是热带雨林的认知模型。像英国的同行一样，他们在思想的环境中成长和发展。某些类型的环境意识很重要，可以用来为环境教育奠定坚实的基础。很明显，教师必须意识到，他们的学生进入课堂时已经带着一些并不总是正确的想法，而且其中一些想法很难改变。建议环境教育的一些要素应该从更小的年龄开始，这样孩子们才能更好地了解他们的环境。
